BYD eMax 7 Residential Charging Guide

By MCN Team • Dec 14, 2025 • 8 min read

eMax 7 Residential Charging Overview

The BYD eMax 7 is making a strong entry into the electric people-mover segment. It features a genuine 7-seater MPV design, advanced Blade Battery Technology, a comfortable and feature-rich cabin, and a cost-friendly positioning. It competes with large ICE vehicles like the Kia Carnival, the Toyota Innova Hycross, and even with other large electric SUVs like the MG Hector Plus due to its affordable price point for a dedicated MPV.

Owning an electric vehicle provides many benefits, the least of which is the astonishing running cost. Compared to a traditional ICE vehicle, the monthly running cost of an EV is 4 to 5x less than the petrol counterpart. Furthermore, features like energy efficiency, high torque, lower maintenance, modern look, and cleaner functioning add to the benefits.

A key convenience of EV ownership is the ability to recharge the vehicle at home. This eliminates the need for frequent trips to fuel stations, waiting in lines, or concerns over fluctuating fuel prices or ethanol content. An EV can be conveniently charged at your residence, much like a mobile phone or laptop—significantly enhancing the comfort and ease of the ownership experience. Let’s explore the different methods for charging your BYD eMax 7 at home.

Charging the BYD eMax 7 with a Standard 16A Outlet

All variants of the BYD eMax 7 (including the Premium variant) come with a portable 3.3 kW charger. This unit is essentially an adapter with a standard 3-pin plug. This plug can be connected to any 16 Ampere (16A) household socket—the type commonly used for high-power appliances such as air conditioners and water heaters. Simply connecting the 3.3 kW charger to the 16A socket initiates the charging process.

Charging Time Calculation

For the 55.4 kWh battery, a full charge (0% to 100%) will take about 17 hours. This is because the 3.3 kW charger draws only 3.3 kW of power per hour, so to fill 55.4 kWh of energy it needs:

55.4 kWh / 3.3 kW ≈ 17 hours

While this is the most straightforward and accessible method for charging the BYD eMax 7, it does present some challenges.

Practical Distance

The 16A socket must be located within a usable distance of the vehicle. This is simple if you live on the ground floor with a nearby socket. For residents in high-rise apartments, charging can be difficult. A potential solution is to work with the building’s electrician to safely extend your home wiring and install a dedicated 16A socket near your parking spot.

Safety Note

Always use high-quality, safety-compliant wiring, such as 3 mm thick, 3-core Polycab wire, and install an MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker) for added security.

This simple charging method, however, has notable disadvantages:

  • Security Risk: A publicly accessible 16A socket is vulnerable to unauthorized use or electricity theft.
  • Inaccurate Energy Tracking: EV usage is mixed into the home meter with other appliances, so tracking is imprecise.
  • Emergency Safety Concerns: Standard socket setups can be risky in heavy rain, thunderstorms, or lightning.
  • Immediate Access: In open parking areas, you may not have quick access to switch off charging in an emergency.

For these reasons, installing a dedicated 3.3 kW AC charging socket—a specialized hardware solution tailored for EV charging—is a safer alternative.

Charging the BYD eMax 7 with a Massive Mobility 3.3 kW AC Charger

The 3.3 kW AC charger is a custom-engineered EV charging point designed to deliver a high-quality charging experience that prioritizes security, functionality, emergency response, and efficiency.

The Massive Mobility 3.3 kW AC charging unit offers a secure 16A outlet that can be remotely managed using a mobile application—your smartphone effectively becomes the remote control for the charger.

Smart Features

The 3.3 kW AC charger and its associated mobile app provide detailed statistics, including electricity consumed, voltage variations, session history, and other practical data that helps you optimize your charging habits.

Cost Control

During power outages, apartment complex generators or backup power sources often charge electricity at 2 to 3x the standard rate. By using the 3.3 kW AC charger, you can customize and remotely control charging, ensuring you only charge when using cost-effective energy.

This solution represents the safest way to charge your EV at your residence.

Charging the BYD eMax 7 with a Massive Mobility 7.4 kW AC Charger

The Massive Mobility 7.4 kW AC charger operates similarly to the 3.3 kW model, but with a significant difference: it draws up to 7.4 kW of power. This allows you to charge your BYD eMax 7 much faster, typically in 8 hours for the 55.4 kWh battery (limited by the car's max AC intake of 7.0 kW). Thus, the 7.4 kW AC charger is often referred to as AC fast charging.

Hardware and Connection

The 7.4 kW AC charger is a permanent hardware unit mounted on the wall near your parking space. It features a long CCS2 charging connector that plugs directly into the BYD eMax 7 to begin charging.

Remote Control and Security

Like its 3.3 kW counterpart, the 7.4 kW AC charger is smartphone-controlled. You can operate the charger remotely from the comfort of your home. Its security features prevent energy theft, as the unit will not activate without authorization from your personal mobile device.

Emergency Response

The remote control feature allows you to instantly switch the charger off in an emergency, mitigating risks during severe weather events like thunderstorms.

In summary, the Massive Mobility 7.4 kW AC charger delivers a seamless, secure, convenient, and premium charging experience. It allows for optimized energy consumption, maximizing the benefits of your electric vehicle without sacrificing comfort or safety.

The Massive Mobility EV charging extension is a unique hardware solution designed to address diverse EV charging requirements.

Extended Reach

Portability

Smart and Secure
